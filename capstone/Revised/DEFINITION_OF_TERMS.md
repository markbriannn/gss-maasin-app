# Definition of Terms

**Administrator** – Refers to the authorized system user responsible for managing the General Service System. The administrator oversees service provider verification, approves registrations and booking requests, monitors system activities through the real-time map view and analytics dashboard, and addresses user concerns.

**Admin Approval Workflow** – The system process where booking requests submitted by clients are reviewed and approved by the administrator before being forwarded to service providers for acceptance or rejection.

**Barangay Clearance** – A document required by the system as part of the service provider verification process to confirm the provider's residency and good standing within the local community.

**Booking Status** – The current state of a service request within the system, progressing through defined stages: Pending → Accepted → Traveling → Arrived → In Progress → Completed.

**Client** – Refers to a registered system user who searches for, requests, and hires service providers for sideline or short-term services through the platform.

**Digital Receipt** – An electronic transaction record generated by the system after service completion, documenting service details, payment amounts, platform fees, and completion status.

**Feedback and Rating** – A system feature that allows clients to assess the performance of service providers by submitting 5-star ratings and written comments after the completion of a service.

**Firebase** – A cloud-based platform by Google used in the system for user authentication (Firebase Authentication), real-time database storage (Firestore), push notifications (Firebase Cloud Messaging), and web hosting (Firebase Hosting).

**General Service System (GSS)** – A web-based and mobile platform developed in this study that connects clients with verified service providers in Maasin City, Southern Leyte, for managing and accessing sideline services.

**Government-Issued ID** – An official identification document required by the system to verify the identity of service providers during the registration process.

**GPS Tracking** – A system feature that uses Global Positioning System technology to display the real-time location of service providers on a map, allowing clients to monitor provider movement and estimated arrival time during active jobs.

**Identity Verification** – The system process in which service providers submit required documents, including a government-issued ID, selfie with ID, Barangay Clearance, and Police Clearance, to ensure authenticity and user safety.

**In-App Messaging** – A built-in communication feature that allows clients and service providers to exchange text messages directly within the platform for service coordination.

**Job Request** – A formal service request submitted by a client through the system, specifying the type of service needed, preferred schedule, service location, and selected payment method.

**Job Tracking** – A system feature that allows both clients and service providers to monitor the status of a job from acceptance through service completion, including real-time GPS location tracking.

**Mobile Application** – The React Native-based version of the General Service System designed for Android and iOS devices, providing native mobile experience with full system functionality.

**Online Payment** – A system function that enables clients to pay service providers through the platform using PayMongo payment gateway, supporting GCash and credit/debit card transactions for secure, transparent, and properly recorded transactions.

**PayMongo** – A Philippine-based payment gateway integrated into the system to process online payments, supporting GCash e-wallet and credit/debit card transactions.

**Platform Service Fee** – A 5% fee added to the client's total payment as system revenue, ensuring service providers receive their full quoted price while supporting platform maintenance and operational costs.

**Police Clearance** – A document required by the system as part of the service provider verification process to confirm that the service provider has no criminal record.

**Push Notification** – Real-time alerts delivered to users' devices through Firebase Cloud Messaging to inform them of booking updates, new messages, provider arrival, and other system events.

**React Native** – A JavaScript framework used to develop the mobile application version of the General Service System, enabling cross-platform development for both Android and iOS devices.

**Selfie with ID** – A photograph of the service provider holding their government-issued ID, required during registration to verify that the person registering matches the submitted identification document.

**Service Categories** – Classifications of services offered on the platform, such as Electrical, Plumbing, Carpentry, Cleaning, and other skilled trades.

**Service Listing** – A system feature that displays the services offered, skills, pricing, availability, ratings, and profile information of verified service providers for client viewing.

**Service Provider** – A registered and verified individual who offers sideline services such as electrical work, plumbing, carpentry, cleaning, or other skilled trades through the system.

**Sideline Services** – Short-term or part-time services offered by individuals through the system to supplement their income.

**System Verification** – The administrative process of reviewing, validating, and approving service provider accounts based on submitted documents and compliance with system requirements.

**User Account** – A registered profile within the system that allows clients and service providers to access features according to their assigned roles, created through email and phone verification.

**Web Application** – The Next.js-based version of the General Service System accessible through internet browsers on desktop and mobile devices without requiring local software installation.

**Web-Based Platform** – A system that operates through internet browsers on desktop and mobile devices without requiring local software installation.
